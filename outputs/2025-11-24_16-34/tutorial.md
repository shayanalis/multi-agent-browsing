## Instructions

Follow these steps to complete the task:

### Step 2

Click on the 'Log in' button located at the top right corner of the Notion homepage to access your account, which will allow you to create your database.

![Step 2](step_001.png)

*You should see a login dialog box prompting you to enter your email and password, bringing you one step closer to accessing your Notion workspace.*

---

### Step 3

Click on the 'Log In' button located at the top right corner of the Notion homepage to access your account and proceed with creating your database.

![Step 3](step_002.png)

*You should see the login interface where you can enter your email and password, bringing you closer to accessing your workspace.*

---

### Step 4

Click on the 'New Database' button located in the left sidebar to initiate the creation of your database.

![Step 4](step_003.png)

*You should see a new dialog box prompting you to choose a database type, bringing you one step closer to setting up your database.*

---

### Step 5

Click on the button labeled 'Dismiss' that appears in the dialog box to close it, which will allow you to proceed with creating your database without distractions.

![Step 5](step_004.png)

*You should see the dialog box disappear, bringing you back to the main Notion interface, and you're now one step closer to creating your database.*

---

### Step 6

Click on the button labeled 'New page' located in the top right corner of the Notion interface to initiate the creation of a new database.

![Step 6](step_005.png)

*You should see a blank page with options to customize your new database, bringing you one step closer to setting it up.*

---

### Step 7

Click on the 'Database' option in the dropdown menu that appears after selecting 'New page' to create a new database structure.

![Step 7](step_006.png)

*You should see a new database interface with options to add columns and customize your database, bringing you one step closer to organizing your information.*

---

### Step 8

Click on 'Empty database' in the template options to create a blank database, which will allow you to start organizing your information from scratch.

![Step 8](step_007.png)

*You're now one step closer to creating your database, and you should see a blank database page with column headers ready for you to customize.*

---

### Step 9

In the text field labeled 'Name', type 'Task Tracker' to designate the name of your new database, which will help you identify its purpose later.

![Step 9](step_008.png)

*You should see the name 'Task Tracker' appear in the database title area, indicating that you've successfully named your database and are one step closer to completing the setup.*

---

### Step 10

Click on the 'Add property' button located at the top of the database interface to include a new property in your database, which will allow you to customize the data structure for your tasks.

![Step 10](step_009.png)

*You should see a new property field appear, allowing you to define the type and name of the property, bringing you closer to fully setting up your database.*

---

### Step 11

Now that you've added a property, click on the 'Status' option in the property type dropdown menu to define the status of your tasks.

![Step 11](step_010.png)

*You should see a list of status options appear, allowing you to categorize your tasks, bringing you closer to completing your database setup.*

---

### Step 12

Click on the 'Status' property in the properties panel to configure the status options for your tasks.

![Step 12](step_011.png)

*You should see a settings menu appear on the right side of the screen, allowing you to customize the status options for your database, bringing you closer to fully setting up your Task Tracker.*

### Step 13

Click on the button labeled 'Add property' in the database interface to include a new property for your tasks.

![Step 13](step_012.png)

---

### Step 14

![Step 14](step_013.png)

### Step 15
